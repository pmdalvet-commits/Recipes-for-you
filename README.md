============================================================
⚠️ TOXIC FOOD WARNING FOR ANIMALS (Recipes-for-you) ⚠️
============================================================

Based on the ingredients used in our recipes (Chocolate, 
Garlic, Onion, Avocado, Dairy, and Salt), here is a 
safety guide for different species:

1. DOGS & CATS
------------------------------------------------------------
X CHOCOLATE (Brigadeiro): Highly toxic! Contains Theobromine. 
  Can cause tremors, seizures, and heart failure.
X GARLIC & ONIONS (Pasta/Rice): Causes oxidative damage to 
  red blood cells, leading to severe anemia.
X AVOCADO (Guacamole): Persin content can cause upset 
  stomachs. The pit is also a major choking hazard.
X DAIRY (Condensed Milk/Cheese): Most adults are lactose 
  intolerant. Causes severe diarrhea and pancreatitis.

2. BIRDS (Parrots, Cockatiels, etc.)
------------------------------------------------------------
X AVOCADO (Guacamole): EXTREMELY FATAL. Can cause heart 
  failure and sudden death within hours.
X CHOCOLATE: Highly toxic to their fast metabolism.
X SALT & GARLIC: Causes kidney failure and dehydration.

3. RABBITS & RODENTS (Guinea Pigs, Hamsters)
------------------------------------------------------------
X AVOCADO: Too high in fat and contains Persin (toxic).
X GARLIC/ONIONS: Can cause blood disorders and 
  anaphylactic shock in small mammals.
X SUGAR/DAIRY: Their digestive systems cannot process 
  complex sugars or milk proteins.

4. REPTILES (Turtles, Lizards)
------------------------------------------------------------
X DAIRY: Reptiles cannot digest lactose at all.
X SALT/SPICES: Causes rapid kidney stress and 
  electrolyte imbalance.
X AVOCADO: High risk of toxicity for most lizards.

5. HORSES & RUMINANTS
------------------------------------------------------------
X AVOCADO: Leaves and fruit are toxic to horses (causes 
  mastitis and colic).
X CHOCOLATE: Considered a prohibited substance in 
  equine competitions and toxic in large amounts.
X GARLIC/ONIONS: Can cause Heinz body anemia in horses 
  and cattle if ingested in significant volume.

------------------------------------------------------------
VET ADVICE: When in doubt, keep human food away from pets!
